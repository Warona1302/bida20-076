Special Events In London

Experience the amazing array of special events in London, including Music Festivals, Notting Hill Carnival, Sport Events, Winter Wonderland, , lastly but not least Royal Parades

Music Festivals

Summer is the season of music festivals in London,
and 2021 promises some serious musical highs to help us all move
on from an exceedingly trying year. From the world’s biggest music icons in
open air theatres, to happy hippie vibes in suburban fields and the very best in
electronic music all found in some of the coolest and unique spaces in the city.
Here’s our guide to London’s best music festivals in 2021.

Notting Hill Carnival 2021

August 2021 (Exact dates to be confirmed)
The streets of west London come alive every August bank holiday weekend with a huge Caribbean party at Europe's biggest street festival:
Notting Hill Carnival Join London’s biggest street party as the Notting Hill Carnival 2021 fills the streets of west London with Caribbean colours, music and flavours. Watch elaborate floats and colourful costumed performers wind their way through the streets in the carnival parade, dance to the sound of steel bands and calypso music, and visit the tempting food stalls along the route.

Sport Event

Find out about spectator sports in London.
Major sporting events in London, including football matches, rugby games
and cricket matches at world-famous venues, from Wembley Stadium
to Wimbledon. In London, a diverse array of athletics stretching from football
to tennis have further granted its city the spotlight throughout the world.
London has hosted the Olympic Games in 1908, 1948,
and most recently in 2012,making it the most frequently chosen city in
modern Olympic history. Other popular sports in London include cricket,
rowing, rugby, basketball, and most recently American Football

Winter Wonderland 2021 in Hyde Park

From November 2021 to January 2022 (Exact dates to be confirmed)
From thrilling fairground rides and ice skating to charming Christmas markets and family shows, join in the seasonal fun at Winter Wonderland in London.
This free-to-enter family event is one of the best things to do in London at Christmas. Featuring a huge selection of festive entertainment and activities, from ice skating and circus shows to a Christmas market and live music, there's enough fun things to do to fill a whole day and evening

Royal Parades

London hosts high profile ceremonial events every year, including The
Queen's Birthday Parade, Beating Retreat, The State Opening of Parliament,
State Visits and more that epitomise the history, pomp and pageantry for
which Britain is famous worldwide, and all those are accompanied by
royal parades.Changing The Queen's Life Guard takes place daily on Horse
Guards Parade where the men and horses of the Household Cavalry Mounted
Regiment stand guard at the
official entrance to Buckingham and St. James's Palace.
